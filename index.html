Dr. Albert Einstein

The life of a genius

Here's a timeline of some of the main events during Albert Einstein's life:

1879
Born in Ulm, in the Kingdom of Württemberg, in the German Empire.
1895
Fails to reach the required standards for the Swiss Federal Polytechnic in Zürich, although he obtains exceptional grades in physics and mathematics. He then attendes the Argovian cantonal school in Aarau, Switzerland.
1896
Passes the Swiss Matura with mostly good grades, including a top grade in physics and mathematical subjects. He then enrolls in the four-year mathematics and physics teaching diploma program at the Zürich Polytechnic.
1900
Was awarded the Zürich Polytechnic teaching diploma.
1902
Starts working at the Federal Office for Intellectual Property, the patent office, in Bern, Switzerland.
1905
Was awarded a PhD by the University of Zürich, with his dissertation entitled, "A New Determination of Molecular Dimensions." That same year, which has been called Einstein's annus mirabilis (miracle year), he published four groundbreaking papers, on the photoelectric effect, Brownian motion, special relativity, and the equivalence of mass and energy.
1908
Was recognized as a leading scientist and was appointed lecturer at the University of Bern.
1909
Was appointed associate professor at the University of Bern.
1914
Returns to the German Empire after being appointed director of the Kaiser Wilhelm Institute for Physics and a professor at the Humboldt University of Berlin, but freed from most teaching obligations.
1921
Was awarded the Nobel Prize in Physics "for his services to Theoretical Physics, and especially for his discovery of the law of the photoelectric effect".
1933
Discovered that the new German government had passed laws barring Jews from holding any official positions, including teaching at universities. He renounces his german passport due to the rise to power of the Nazis under Germany's new chancellor, Adolf Hitler, and moves to the US.
1939
Participates in the Manhattan Project, which leads to the development of the first atomic bomb.
1955
Dies at the age of 76 after an internal bleeding caused by the rupture of an abdominal aortic aneurysm.
